## Charcoal pencil on newsprint

This composition was partly inspired by your recent post showing a sharp contrast in a developed portrait and much more sparse sketch for the rest of the drawing. I think I've made a lot of improvements in rendering core shadows as a connected "river" rather than disjointed marks. I struggled a little bit with interpreting the complex shadows around the eye on the shadow side, and maybe the likeness isn't fully accurate to the reference, but I'm satisfied with the value design and composition as a whole.

## Procreate portrait

This one is fun because I commissioned a local model who joins the life drawing sessions I host for a custom portrait reference pack that I can use for my own practice but also for classes. There are so many great references out there, but it's nice to have a pack from a model I work with directly and can take my requests into account.

I tried to apply your feedback about applying more subtle transitions in the lights for a portrait between highlights and soft midtones. I like the graphic energy of the hair rendering, but I'm not sure about the values. I might need to do more hair studies...

## Another project!

I think you saw this on Instagram, but I'm writing a life drawing book! It's primarily for my own use in guided life drawing sessions as a repository of simple exercises I can pull from and share with students.

But it's also designed to be a handbook you can carry with you to life drawing sessions as an inspirational reference to keep your practice fresh and well-rounded. In between exercises I'm mixing in short chapters on the creative practice of life drawing beyond the technical practice. I find there are a lot of figure drawing books that are helpful references for things like the basics of materials, common construction patterns for all the parts of the body, and a few high level exercises. And then there are books like Henry Yan's that are pretty heavy on sharing full processes for life drawing in various pose lengths and styles. But I haven't found many that collect a wide variety of exercises targeting specific skills for an ongoing life drawing practice (like the egg rendering portrait exercise you shared on Proko and on Patreon!). So that's what this book is. It's very rough and changing quickly as I write, but I'd love to share it with you when it's in a more intelligible state.
